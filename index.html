Section 5 - Final Interpretation & Insights

So what?

What the data reveals

Asian and Hispanic employees are not only increasing — they are growing faster than Massachusetts trends.

Black and Two or More Races groups, however, are growing slower than the state’s population growth. This indicates underrepresentation in growth pace relative to community demographics.

Asian: pace ahead Hispanic: pace ahead Black: pace behind Two+: pace behind

Key equity insight

Hispanic and Two or More Races groups hold ~1.5× higher population share in Massachusetts compared to Black and Asian populations. This means these two groups have the largest more growth potential in the labor market.

Labor market signal Future talent pool

Overall story

  • Our department is making progress in minority representation.
  • But the pace of growth is not consistent across groups.
  • To stay aligned, strengthening recruitment and retention strategies for Black employees and Two or More Races employees will be essential going forward.
