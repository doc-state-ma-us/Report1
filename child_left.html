Veteran representation trend (2022–2025)

This view tracks both veteran headcount and veterans as a share of the total workforce. Both measures decline steadily from 2022 to 2025.

Key takeaways:
  • Veteran headcount declines: 488 → 479 → 459 → 428.
  • Veteran share drops: 16.9% → 16.0% → 14.2% → 13.3%.
  • Overall implication: It’s not just a headcount drop - Veterans are becoming a smaller share of the workforce, meaning overall workforce growth (or shifts in hiring) is outpacing veteran presence.

Minority headcount by race (2022–2025).

1) Minority headcount by race (totals)

This bar chart explains the upward trajectory in minority representation observed earlier. Across MSP, most racial groups show sustained positive growth over time.

What this suggests:
  • Minority representation is increasing across multiple race categories.
  • Compared to the broader Secretariat trend for Black, Hispanic, and Asian, MSP appears stronger.
  • This raises a key question: why was there a sharp jump between 2023 and 2024 that we did not see at the Secretariat level?

To explain the jump and check whether growth is consistent, we look at the year-over-year change.

2) Year-over-year change (what drove the jump?)

This heatmap shows the net change compared to the previous year (not total headcount). It answers: where did the 2023→2024 jump come from?

Answer: The 2023→2024 jump is primarily driven by Hispanic growth.

What the heatmap reveals:
  • Growth rate slows in the most recent year (2024→2025).
  • Growth is not uniform across racial groups—some accelerate while others slow.
  • The most broadly distributed (“most diverse”) growth occurred in 2022→2023.

At a high level, shifts in growth patterns can reflect changes in outreach focus, engagement, or pipeline effectiveness over time.

Key question: How can we combine best efforts to drive balanced, sustained minority growth?

3) Where minority representation concentrates (EEO categories)

This view shows how minority representation is distributed across EEO job categories. It helps answer: where minority representation concentrates and where additional outreach may be needed.

How to read this:
  • Across all years, minority representation is dominated by: Protective Service: Sworn, Professionals, Officials and Administrators, Protective Service: Non-Sworn.
  • Some categories show consistently low totals and limited change: Technicians, Service Maintenance, Office/Clerical.
