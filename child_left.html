Minority distribution by race (2022–2025.

1) Minority distribution by race

This bar chart explains the upward trajectory in Minority representation that we observed earlier. Across MSP, most racial groups show positive upward movement over time.

What this suggests:
  • Minority representation is increasing across multiple race categories.
  • Compared to the broader EOPSS trend for Black, Hispanic, and Asian groups, the MSP pattern appears stronger.
  • This raises a key question: why was there a sharp jump between 2023 and 2024 that we did not see at the EOPSS level?

To explain the jump and check whether growth is consistent, we look at the year-over-year change.

2) Growth in numbers (year-over-year change)

The heat map breaks down net change by race across three year window. This provides the clearest answer to the question: where did the 2023→2024 jump come from?

Ans: The 2023→2024 jump is primarily driven by Hispanic growth.

What the heat map reveals:
  • Growth rate decreases in the most recent year (2024→2025).
  • Growth is not uniform across racial groups, some groups accelerate while others slow.
  • The most broadly distributed (“most diverse”) growth occurred in 2022→2023.

On the bigger picture, changes in minority growth patterns may reflect differences in outreach focus, engagement, or pipeline effectiveness across time.

Key question: Can we combine the best efforts together to drive balanced, sustained minority growth?

3) Minority representation by EEO Job Category

This view shows how minority representation is distributed across EEO job categories. It helps answer: where does minority representation concentrate and where might additional outreach be needed.

How to read this:
  • Across all years, minority representation is dominated by four EEO job categories: Protective Service, Professionals, Officials and Administrators, Protective Service: Non-Sworn
  • Categories exhibit flat or declining patterns across years: Technicians, Service Maintenance, Office/Clerical
